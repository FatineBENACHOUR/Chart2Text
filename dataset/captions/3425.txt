With over half a million students , comprehensive schools accounted for the largest educational institution in Finland in 2018 . These schools provide compulsory education and include primary schools ( grades 1 to 6 ) and secondary schools ( grades 7 to 9 ) . The region of Uusimaa with the largest amount of pupils Uusimaa , the region with the capital city Helsinki , encompasses the highest number of comprehensive schools in the country in 2018 .
